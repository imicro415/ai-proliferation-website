Artificial Intelligence has been a developing crisis between the United States and China emerging in the past few years. The intensifying race towards better and more capable AI agents and models is one that that shoudl be carefully watched by the most obvious contenders in the race, but also self-interested players on the sidelines. The technological arms race between China and the US is one that could prove disastarous for the parties involved as well as potential collateral states that could be dragged in due to their potential obligations as allies to either major party involved. Observers have characterized this race in economic, security, and normative terms, with some emphasizing realist power competition and others highlighting liberal institutional cooperation.
